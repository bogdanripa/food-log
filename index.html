Food Log

Food Log is a personal meal and drink tracker that runs as a Claude skill. You describe (or photograph) what you ate, and it logs the meal — with estimated macros — to a Google Sheet in your own Drive.

Later you can ask Claude questions grounded in your history: what's my average weekly protein intake?, how much alcohol have I had this month?, or based on what I logged this week, what should I have for lunch? I'm at [restaurant].

How to install it

  1. Download the skill bundle:
    Download food-tracking.zip
  2. Open Claude.
  3. Go to Customize → Skills → New Skill → Upload and drop in the zip you just downloaded.
  4. Open a new chat — this is where you'll log your food and drinks from now on.

Logging a meal

In that chat, just say what you ate. For example:

I had a lasagna for lunch, please log it.

Or even simpler — upload a photo of your plate or drink and tell Claude to log it.

The first time, Claude will ask you to sign in with Google (one-time setup). After that, every entry is appended to a Google Sheet the skill creates in your Drive, and any photos go into a folder alongside it.

Initially your sheet will have a single row — what you just logged. As you keep logging over the days and weeks, the sheet fills up with your eating history.

Asking questions about your history

Once you have a few days of data, you can ask Claude things like:

  • What's my average weekly protein intake?
  • What's missing from my diet lately?
  • How much alcohol have I been drinking?
  • Based on what I logged this week, what should I have for lunch? I'm at [restaurant].

Claude reads your sheet directly to answer.

Where your data lives

Your data lives in your own Google account: one spreadsheet the app creates for you, and one Drive folder for photos. The app never sees any other files in your Drive.
